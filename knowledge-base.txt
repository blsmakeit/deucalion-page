=== WHAT IS DEUCALION ===
Deucalion is one of Europe's most powerful supercomputers, hosted at MACC (Minho Advanced Computing Centre) in Braga, Portugal. It is part of the EuroHPC Joint Undertaking, a European initiative to build world-class supercomputing infrastructure across Europe. Inaugurated in 2023, Deucalion achieves peak performance exceeding 30 petaflops, ranking among the top supercomputers in Europe. The system is named after the Greek mythological figure Deucalion, the son of Prometheus, symbolizing Portugal's connection to fire, light, and the forging of a new future through technology. It represents a landmark achievement for Portuguese scientific and industrial competitiveness.

=== TECHNICAL SPECIFICATIONS ===
Deucalion's architecture is designed for both traditional HPC workloads and modern AI/ML tasks:
- AMD EPYC processors for CPU workloads (high core count, large memory capacity)
- AMD Instinct MI250X GPUs for AI and parallel computing (each GPU features 128GB HBM2e memory)
- High-speed InfiniBand HDR interconnect for ultra-low latency node communication
- Petabyte-scale parallel storage (Lustre filesystem) for high-throughput I/O
- Approximately 30 petaflops peak performance (combined CPU + GPU)
- Hundreds of computing nodes across CPU-only and GPU-accelerated partitions
- Water cooling systems for maximum energy efficiency
- Located in MACC's purpose-built data center in Braga, Portugal
- Supports ROCm (AMD's GPU programming platform), CUDA-compatible workloads, MPI, OpenMP
- Pre-installed scientific software stack: OpenFOAM, GROMACS, NAMD, TensorFlow, PyTorch, LAMMPS

=== MAKEIT PROJECTS WITH DEUCALION ===

Project 1: EV Aerodynamics Optimization
Industry: Automotive
Challenge: An electric vehicle manufacturer needed to dramatically reduce aerodynamic drag to maximize driving range and competitiveness in the European EV market. Traditional wind tunnel testing was too slow and expensive to iterate effectively.
Solution: MakeIt designed a massively parallel CFD workflow using OpenFOAM with AMD GPU-optimized solvers. We ran over 1,200 parallel simulations on Deucalion, exploring thousands of geometric parameter combinations across the vehicle body, underbody, and wheel arches using a Design of Experiments approach coupled with gradient-based optimization.
Impact: Achieved 8.3% reduction in drag coefficient, translating directly to a 12% increase in driving range. Total time from project kickoff to final design: 3 weeks, compared to 6 months using traditional sequential testing. The client brought the optimized design to production within one year.
Technologies: OpenFOAM, ParaView, PyFoam, custom Python optimization framework, MPI, AMD ROCm

Project 2: AI-Accelerated Drug Discovery
Industry: Pharmaceutical
Challenge: A pharmaceutical company needed to screen millions of small molecule candidates for binding affinity to a target protein associated with a rare neurological disease. Traditional in-vitro screening would have taken over 18 months and cost tens of millions of euros.
Solution: MakeIt developed a hybrid pipeline combining molecular dynamics simulations with a deep learning model. The ML model — trained on 50 terabytes of simulation data generated on Deucalion's GPU nodes — learned to predict binding affinity in milliseconds instead of hours. We then used the model to screen a virtual library of 4 million compounds.
Impact: Identified 47 high-potential molecular candidates in just 3 weeks. Three of these candidates are currently in pre-clinical testing, with one entering Phase I clinical trials. The approach reduced the screening cost by over 90% compared to traditional methods.
Technologies: GROMACS, TensorFlow, PyTorch, RDKit, custom graph neural network (GNN), AMD MI250X GPUs

Project 3: Wind Farm Layout Optimization
Industry: Renewable Energy
Challenge: An energy developer planning a 200-turbine offshore wind farm on the Portuguese Atlantic coast needed to optimize turbine placement to maximize annual energy production while minimizing wake interference effects between turbines.
Solution: MakeIt implemented a coupled simulation-optimization framework on Deucalion. We ran thousands of high-fidelity wake simulations using the FLORIS framework enhanced with LES (Large Eddy Simulation) corrections, combined with a genetic algorithm that explored over 500,000 distinct layout configurations across varying wind rose scenarios.
Impact: The optimized layout delivers 23% more annual energy production compared to the initial engineering estimate. At the planned capacity, this represents approximately €8 million in additional annual revenue over the farm's 25-year lifespan. The client is now using MakeIt's framework for two additional offshore projects.
Technologies: FLORIS, OpenFOAM (LES), NREL's WISDEM, custom genetic algorithm, Python parallel framework, MPI

Project 4: Real-Time Financial Risk Engine
Industry: Banking and Finance
Challenge: A major Portuguese bank's overnight portfolio risk assessments required 4 hours of computation, preventing real-time risk monitoring. The system also couldn't handle the complex cross-asset correlations and stress scenarios required by new regulatory frameworks (Basel IV).
Solution: MakeIt redesigned the risk engine as a GPU-accelerated Monte Carlo application running as a distributed parallel workload on Deucalion. The new system generates and analyzes 10 million market scenarios in minutes, with full cross-asset correlation matrices and jump-diffusion processes.
Impact: Computation time reduced from 4 hours to 18 minutes — a 13x speedup. The bank can now run 10x more scenarios, dramatically improving risk model accuracy. Estimated annual infrastructure savings of €2.1 million by replacing legacy on-premise hardware. The system handles intraday risk updates that were previously impossible.
Technologies: C++, CUDA/ROCm, Python, MPI, custom GPU-optimized Monte Carlo kernels, HDF5, Apache Arrow

Project 5: Atlantic Ocean Circulation Model
Industry: Environmental Research / Government
Challenge: Portuguese environmental research institutions needed a high-resolution model of North Atlantic circulation patterns to study climate change impacts on Portugal's coastline, including sea level rise, marine temperature anomalies, and fisheries migration.
Solution: MakeIt deployed and configured a coupled NEMO (ocean) + WRF (atmosphere) model on Deucalion, achieving 3x higher spatial resolution than any previous regional model of this area — from 10km to 3km grid spacing. The coupled model ran 50-year hindcast and 30-year projection simulations.
Impact: The model revealed previously unknown mesoscale ocean circulation patterns affecting the Portuguese coastal upwelling system. Results have been published in three top-tier oceanography journals and are directly informing the Portuguese national coastal adaptation plan. The dataset is now openly available to the European scientific community.
Technologies: NEMO v4.2, WRF 4.5, Python (xarray, Dask), CDO, NCO, MPI, NetCDF, 128 GPU nodes

Your company could benefit from one if you need to: process massive datasets that exceed your current infrastructure capacity, run complex physics simulations (aerodynamics, structural mechanics, fluid flow), train large AI or machine learning models, or explore optimization problems with millions of variables. If your engineers are waiting hours or days for simulation results, or if you have a research question that seems computationally "impossible," that's a strong signal that HPC could transform your work.
